# When to Use "ga" (が) Instead of "wa" (は) in Japanese

| Function                     | "Ga" (が)                                                                               | "Wa" (は)                           | Example with "Ga"                                                                        |
| ---------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Marking new information**  | Introduces new or unknown information                                                   | Marks known information or topics   | 誰が来ましたか？ (_Dare ga kimashita ka?_) "Who came?"                                   |
| **Exhaustive listing**       | Identifies something from a group                                                       | Contrasts topics                    | 田中さんが社長です (_Tanaka-san ga shachō desu_) "Tanaka is the president" (identifying) |
| **Neutral description**      | Used in neutral descriptions of events/states                                           | Used for topic-comment structures   | 雨が降っています (_Ame ga futte imasu_) "It's raining"                                   |
| **With certain expressions** | Used with specific verbs: ある/いる (_aru/iru_), 好き (_suki_), 欲しい (_hoshii_), etc. | Not typically used with these verbs | 猫が好きです (_Neko ga suki desu_) "I like cats"                                         |
| **In subordinate clauses**   | Often used in subordinate clauses                                                       | Less common in subordinate clauses  | 私が作った料理 (_Watashi ga tsukutta ryōri_) "The food that I made"                      |
| **Focus/emphasis**           | Emphasizes the subject                                                                  | Emphasizes the predicate            | 私が行きます (_Watashi ga ikimasu_) "I will go" (emphasizing who will go)                |
| **With questions**           | Used with question words (who, what, etc.)                                              | Rarely used with question words     | 何が面白いですか？ (_Nani ga omoshiroi desu ka?_) "What is interesting?"                 |

# Expressions That Require "ga" (が) Instead of "wa" (は) in Japanese

| Category                     | Expressions/Verbs                                                                                                                     | Examples                                                                                                                                                           |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Existence Verbs**          | ある (aru) - to exist (inanimate)<br>いる (iru) - to exist (animate)                                                                  | 本**が**あります<br>Hon **ga** arimasu<br>"There is a book"<br><br>猫**が**います<br>Neko **ga** imasu<br>"There is a cat"                                         |
| **Sensory/Perceptual Verbs** | 見える (mieru) - to be visible<br>聞こえる (kikoeru) - to be audible<br>分かる (wakaru) - to understand<br>感じる (kanjiru) - to feel | 山**が**見えます<br>Yama **ga** miemasu<br>"I can see the mountain"<br><br>音楽**が**聞こえます<br>Ongaku **ga** kikoemasu<br>"I can hear music"                   |
| **Potential Expressions**    | できる (dekiru) - can do<br>~られる/~れる (~rareru/~reru) - potential form                                                            | 日本語**が**できます<br>Nihongo **ga** dekimasu<br>"I can speak Japanese"<br><br>漢字**が**読めます<br>Kanji **ga** yomemasu<br>"I can read kanji"                 |
| **Preference/Desire**        | 好き (suki) - to like<br>嫌い (kirai) - to dislike<br>欲しい (hoshii) - to want<br>～たい (~tai) - want to do                         | コーヒー**が**好きです<br>Kōhī **ga** suki desu<br>"I like coffee"<br><br>新しい車**が**欲しいです<br>Atarashii kuruma **ga** hoshii desu<br>"I want a new car"    |
| **Emotion/Condition**        | 怖い (kowai) - scary<br>楽しい (tanoshii) - fun<br>悲しい (kanashii) - sad<br>嬉しい (ureshii) - happy                                | 幽霊**が**怖いです<br>Yūrei **ga** kowai desu<br>"Ghosts are scary"<br><br>パーティー**が**楽しかったです<br>Pātī **ga** tanoshikatta desu<br>"The party was fun"  |
| **Need/Necessity**           | 必要 (hitsuyō) - necessary<br>要る (iru) - to need                                                                                    | お金**が**必要です<br>Okane **ga** hitsuyō desu<br>"Money is necessary"<br><br>塩**が**要ります<br>Shio **ga** irimasu<br>"I need salt"                            |
| **Ability/Skill**            | 上手 (jōzu) - good at<br>下手 (heta) - bad at                                                                                         | テニス**が**上手です<br>Tenisu **ga** jōzu desu<br>"I'm good at tennis"<br><br>料理**が**下手です<br>Ryōri **ga** heta desu<br>"I'm bad at cooking"                |
| **Physical Sensations**      | 痛い (itai) - painful<br>かゆい (kayui) - itchy<br>眠い (nemui) - sleepy                                                              | 頭**が**痛いです<br>Atama **ga** itai desu<br>"My head hurts"<br><br>目**が**眠いです<br>Me **ga** nemui desu<br>"My eyes are sleepy"                              |
| **Happening/Occurrence**     | ある (aru) - to happen/occur<br>起きる (okiru) - to occur                                                                             | 事故**が**ありました<br>Jiko **ga** arimashita<br>"An accident happened"<br><br>地震**が**起きました<br>Jishin **ga** okimashita<br>"An earthquake occurred"       |
| **Sufficiency**              | 足りる (tariru) - to be enough<br>十分 (jūbun) - enough/sufficient                                                                    | 時間**が**足りません<br>Jikan **ga** tarimasen<br>"There's not enough time"<br><br>お金**が**十分あります<br>Okane **ga** jūbun arimasu<br>"There is enough money" |

These expressions typically require "ga" because they mark the direct relationship between the subject and predicate, often describing inherent qualities, states, or subjective experiences rather than making statements about topics.
